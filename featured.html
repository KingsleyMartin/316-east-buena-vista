Featured in Publications

Celebrating the Wilson house restoration and gardens through national magazine features and local media recognition.

The historic Wilson house and its remarkable gardens have been featured in prestigious national and regional publications, recognizing the property's architectural significance, innovative restoration work, and exceptional high-desert horticulture. These features document the evolution of the property through different ownership periods and celebrate the ongoing commitment to historic preservation.

National Publication April 2008

Phoenix Home & Garden Magazine

"High-Desert Oasis: A Santa Fe Garden"

During the Waldron era, Phoenix Home & Garden magazine featured the property in their April 2008 issue, celebrating the remarkable transformation of the historic gardens into a lush, year-round oasis in Santa Fe's challenging high-desert climate.

Feature Highlights

  • Garden Restoration Excellence: Detailed coverage of Aaron and Chantal Waldron's comprehensive garden restoration, including the rebuilt greenhouse, reestablished kitchen garden, and meticulously maintained terraced plantings.
  • Water Feature Integration: Extensive documentation of the property's innovative stream and pond system, creating a serene water landscape that enhances the garden's microclimate while providing habitat for wildlife.
  • Year-Round Color Strategy: Exploration of the garden's diverse plant palette featuring over 40 species of perennials, ornamental grasses, and flowering shrubs selected for continuous seasonal interest.
  • Courtyard Living: Interior and exterior photography showcasing how the house and gardens function as integrated living spaces, with covered portals connecting indoor rooms to outdoor garden rooms.
  • Historic Context: Recognition of the property's Prairie-style architecture and its significance within Santa Fe's early 20th-century residential development.

Notable Quote: The article emphasized the garden's remarkable water retention, noting that "dense plantings and natural wind protection allowed the soil to hold water to an unusual degree"—a crucial factor in creating such lush gardens in Santa Fe's arid environment.

National Publication July 2005

Sunset Magazine

"Santa Fe Garden Tour"

Sunset Magazine, renowned for its coverage of Western living and gardens, featured the property in their July 2005 issue as part of a curated Santa Fe garden tour, highlighting the innovative approaches to high-desert landscaping.

Feature Highlights

  • Sustainable Water Management: Focus on the property's historic well system and water-efficient irrigation practices adapted for modern use.
  • Native Plant Integration: Documentation of how native New Mexico species were integrated with cultivated garden plants to create a sustainable, climate-appropriate landscape.
  • Outdoor Living Spaces: Coverage of the property's multiple garden rooms, including intimate courtyard spaces and expansive terraced areas.
  • Regional Garden Excellence: Recognition among Sunset's selection of the finest gardens in Santa Fe, chosen for innovation and beauty.

National Publication September 2006

Sunset Magazine

"Great Gardens of the Southwest"

Returning to feature the property again in September 2006, Sunset Magazine included the gardens in their "Great Gardens of the Southwest" coverage, emphasizing the property's role as a model for sustainable high-desert horticulture.

Feature Highlights

  • Seasonal Garden Evolution: Documentation of how the gardens transform through different seasons, providing year-round interest and color.
  • Fruit Tree Heritage: Recognition of the property's historic fruit trees, some dating to the 1920s Charlotte Wilson era, and their continued productivity.
  • Water-Wise Techniques: Detailed coverage of irrigation strategies that maintain lush gardens while respecting water conservation principles.
  • Garden Design Principles: Analysis of the terraced design that maximizes water efficiency while creating distinct garden zones with different microclimates.

Design Recognition: Sunset praised the garden's "thoughtful integration of formal and informal elements" and its demonstration that "Southwest gardens can be both water-wise and beautifully lush."

Regional Publication

Santa Fe Living

"Historic Preservation & Modern Living"

Santa Fe Living magazine featured the property, highlighting the successful integration of historic preservation with contemporary lifestyle needs. The article explored how the house and gardens maintain their early 20th-century character while serving modern residential requirements.

Feature Highlights

  • Architectural Heritage: Detailed examination of the house's Prairie-style design elements and their significance in Santa Fe's architectural history.
  • Preservation Philosophy: Coverage of restoration approaches that honor original design while incorporating necessary modern systems and conveniences.
  • Interior Design Integration: Exploration of how period-appropriate furnishings and contemporary pieces coexist in the historic spaces.
  • Garden-House Connection: Analysis of the seamless indoor-outdoor living made possible by the property's courtyard design and portal spaces.
  • Community Context: Discussion of the property's role within Santa Fe's historic preservation movement and its contribution to the city's architectural heritage.

Preservation Impact: The article emphasized the property's demonstration that "historic preservation doesn't require sacrificing comfort or style—it enriches daily living by connecting us to our architectural heritage."

Media Recognition & Impact

These magazine features represent more than media attention—they document the property's evolution and its role as a model for historic preservation and sustainable high-desert horticulture. The recognition from national publications like Phoenix Home & Garden and Sunset Magazine, along with local Santa Fe Living coverage, has helped establish the property as a significant example of how historic buildings can be preserved and adapted for contemporary living while maintaining their architectural integrity and historical character.
